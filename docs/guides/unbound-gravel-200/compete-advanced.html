1 · Training Plan Brief

Welcome to Unbound Gravel 200 Training

This plan isn't generic. It's built for Unbound Gravel 200—its 200 miles, Flint Hills gravel roads, 11,000 feet of elevation gain, and what it'll take to be out there for 10-15 hours.

Unbound isn't a race you survive by accident. It's a race you prepare for—or it prepares you for a very long day.

By the time you roll to the start, you'll know you're ready.

What Makes This Plan Different

Built for your ability level.

You're on the Advanced version (Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced). The load and intensity match where you are right now.

Built for your schedule.

This is the COMPETE tier, designed around 12-18 hours. The week fits into your life so you can actually complete it.

Built for this race.

The sessions, long rides, and progressions target the key demands of Unbound Gravel 200: Extreme heat and humidity, Long distance requiring exceptional endurance, Technical gravel sections requiring bike handling skills.

How This Plan Is Structured

12 Weeks, 4 Phases

  • Weeks 1-3: Base Phase — Building aerobic foundation and endurance
  • Weeks 4-7: Build Phase — Adding intensity, developing race-specific fitness
  • Weeks 8-10: Peak Phase — Maximum training load and sharpening
  • Weeks 11-12: Taper Phase — Reducing volume while maintaining intensity, arriving fresh
Phase Overview – Volume & Intensity Progression
(12-week bar chart showing training load progression across Base, Build, Peak, and Taper phases)

Weekly Structure

5-6 sessions per week: 2-3 intervals, 2-3 endurance rides, 1 long ride, 1 recovery

Recovery Weeks

Every third or fourth week is a recovery week with 30-40% reduced volume. Recovery makes you fast. Don't skip these.

What You Need to Succeed

Non-negotiables:

  1. A power meter
  2. A heart rate monitor
  3. A bike set up correctly for gravel racing
  4. Commitment to the full 12 weeks
  5. Willingness to follow the plan as written
  6. TrainingPeaks Premium (so your workouts sync to your device)
  7. Smart trainer for indoor workouts

A Note on Compliance vs. Perfection

You won't execute this plan perfectly. No one does.

Life happens. Work intrudes. Illness strikes. Weather doesn't cooperate. Kids need attention. That's reality.

The goal isn't perfection---it's consistency. Complete 85-90% of the prescribed workouts, and you'll arrive at Unbound Gravel 200 prepared. Complete 95%+, and you'll be exceptionally prepared.

Miss multiple weeks, skip the hard workouts, or constantly substitute "easier" options, and the plan won't work. Not because the plan failed---because you didn't do it.

Be honest with yourself about compliance.
